The Feeling of Achieving the Most Important Goal in University

Posted on 07/07/2019

Goals, goals, goals. Everyone has their own specific short or long-term rather realistic or unrealistic goals. This can vary depending on their situation, lifestyle, desire and vision. And, of course most people with dedication will achieve their goals, not all but some. Hence, I wanted to share how I achieved the most important goal in my university with the hopes of inspiring the fellow students to commit on whatever they are trying to achieve.

So, it was the first day of my 3rd year, and the classic question came up from my tutor “Have you guys struck a thought of your final year projects?”. Some already figured out what they were going to work on. I was one of them. I had this one very idea which I wanted to work on in an Android platform where at the time I had no experience of. Thus, I wanted to wait a little, while my brain can come up with other project ideas that I could potentially do on a platform where I have some experience at the least. However, I subconsciously was so into wanting to work on this project that I knew this will be the most fun I will have in creating an app because for some reason I developed this passion for wanting to work on Android apps. And the main reason why I demanded for this very specific idea was because I had it in mind in summertime already.

After a casual walk on the way home from a hardcore session at gym, an idea of my little brother being kidnapped came around for some weird reason. I had this for couple of days and figured out that I can build an application based around Emergency notification service. Hence, I wanted to keep this idea for my final year project and then start learning android alongside with developing too. My tutor tried to enforce the idea of working on a project in a platform where you have experience in which was mostly what university have taught. And, he wasn’t wrong. Rationally, it makes sense not to spend any more time in learning because this is where you showcase what you’ve learnt throughout the academic years. However, in order for me to fully get the satisfaction of achieving the optimal goal at the end of the year, as a means to achieving a certain mark in First class, I had to go with something that I will be happy and passionate of doing.

So, I went with the idea, started the dissertation by researching about the project behind the application in the first term then fully banged on developing the application whilst learning at the same time for most days in the week in the second term. Though, things weren’t going so well… I had a one-to-one private session every week, just like everyone else, with our tutor to ensure that both sides know the progress and are on the right track. Most of these sessions went with quite a roasting session by my tutor about how I will be passing the year but not getting over 2.2 and that it was just so average. Maybe I couldn’t express the idea fully or wasn’t really able to show to progression in those sessions or it was just the fact that he didn’t want me to be in my comfortable zone and push me to the point where I can be like ‘yeah, I proved him wrong’. Yet, he showed me how I can do some things to achieve more marks. And to be honest, I believe it all worked out at the end.

Finally, I submitted my 15k+ words dissertation alongside with my working Android application then settled to wait for the results. I somehow had the feeling of achieving what I wished and worked for after 3 years because I fully knew I put in the time and effort into it in order to accomplish “the” goal. But I also had negative thoughts which I couldn’t hold my self not to think about it, where I doubted my abilities and asked the question “what if?” … What if couldn’t make it, what if I couldn’t achieve it, what if I didn’t work as hard as I thought I did? As the time was flying past, the tension and the anxiety was getting stronger. But it all ended on that day, just like any other day when I was checking my university portal to see if the results been uploaded or not. I was happy first of all, but also excited, had goose bumps and felt proud of myself accomplishing what I worked for throughout the years and not just because I received the first class honours, but it all did worth it after the sacrifices I made and being very consistent and determined even after such setbacks and failures of not being making progress. It made me realise, might sound cliché but you can achieve whatever you put in your mind, and keeping the 3 P’s (Patience, Positivity, Persistence) under your hood will make you succeed if you have the vision and the mission of what you will be doing no matter what the goal is.
