How HackerRank helped me

Posted on 27/01/2019

I was fresh out of University, got my degree in my hand and wondering if I was capable enough for a developer job. At that time, I didn’t feel like I was ready to start working hence I took some time off focusing on my programming skills. Before I found out HackerRank, I worked on personal projects and deployed my website to showcase my portfolio so I stand out from other candidates where the competition in this industry for a graduate is very high. After a month, I decided to start applying for jobs and focus on improving my skills in Algorithms and all kinds of data structures as I knew it will help me out in interviews alongside with general development of my technical skills.

Then I found out HackerRank, I started practicing and solving algorithmic problems 2 days a week, also I had to work on other projects so I needed to manage my time in this way. At first, even though I have been programming for 3 years, worked on several university and personal projects, I struggled with solving basic algorithmic problems. This made me realise how unpractical I was and it sort of demoralised me as I reckon this was normal because of the fact that I was confident in my capabilities. However, I had to push through and stay committed so, within a short period of time, basics were a piece of cake as it should’ve been from day one. Consequently I started with medium problems and by this time I landed my first-ever interview for a developer role and got rejected.

I kept on working on HackerRank and my projects until I landed my first-ever developer role after 4 months of job hunting and 7 rejections. What I can suggest to fresh technical graduates is that, if you want a developer career and feel like you are not ready for it, then all you will be doing is delaying because you can never be 100% ready for anything, instead start applying for jobs and work on your technical knowledge which I developed an Android application just for this purpose - Interview Revision and adding practicality which HackerRank can help you upon. There are other similar platforms out to help you develop your programming skills but HackerRank was the one that I found useful.

I mainly used Interview Preparation Kit and Problem Solving categories which I think you should be start working on solving the problems and develop your algorithmic knowledge.

  • Algorithms
  • Data Structures
  • Interview Revision
  • Commitment
