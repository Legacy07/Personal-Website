Why I think Universities Are a Waste of Time and Money for Most Courses

Posted on 12/02/2019

Before I start my rant, I would like to specify that I base this opinion off my personal university experience which I had some great time in and from my friends whom were and some still are in university. Thus I wouldn’t want to talk about subjects that I have no knowledge or experience. Hence why I’m posting this.

So, I studied Computer Science at University of Greenwich for full 3 years. I have met quite some people, in fact variety of people with different personalities, mentalities, visions and backgrounds. I was never a person who searched for a lot of friends so I stayed in a small bubble where I and the others had similar personalities, vision and good humour because the less is always the better.

I’ve made quite some observations as I was focusing in my studies. I found out that there are some individuals who considered University because of its so-called lifestyle and as a way to hit on chicks in library rather than developing themselves and achieving a decent grade because realistically anyone can pass with some help using their “Uni friends”. Don’t get me wrong, Universities are great; from their lively, friendly environment to capturing unique experiences and memories as you won’t go to a University more than once (at least for most of us). However, you got to have some vision and mission to go along with so you aware of what you’re up to and what you will be doing after achieving a certain grade which should be the highest grade you can possible get. If you are aiming for a pass or a 2.2 then not only you are wasting £27k plus all the other maintenance loans etc… and 3 years of your life but also you are wasting your bright future which I don’t think it’s so bright in the first place if you have this mentality.

Anyways, I don’t want to side track, I will probably write a separate blog post on why you shouldn’t be like one of those I mentioned. Nonetheless, there are two main reasons to why I think universities are waste of money and time. The first is that you can most probably be in a better state with no debt if you start with apprenticeship and within 3 years of experience, you will be more than qualified to work anywhere in your field and most likely to stay in the company you started with. Instead of being in a job hunt for months with the degree in your hand staying home depressed. Courses that cover Business, Computing, Construction, Music, Media and any other field that has an alternative with pure experience, you should without of a doubt should try find a place to start working. Unless it’s anything major and cannot be achieved with experiences like Medical, Science-related and architectural courses, Universities for the former courses are at a current state is full on waste.

From my own experience, my University helped lay the foundation of my general soft-skills and technical skills to a certain extent. I certainly don’t feel like it worth £9k for the materials that universities teach and provide. Just like few years ago, if the cost of Universities were £3k, this blog post wouldn’t have existed.

Although, if you are in a developing country, you won’t have the apprenticeship levels as we have in countries like the UK because the employers in those countries a tend to use ambitious young ones as slaves so it will be a better option to go to a University so you have a better chance of getting into an industry or trying your luck abroad.

But if you are reading this whilst at University, aim for higher than the highest grade possible and try your best to achieve that and never forget; “It’s not what University provides, It’s what you can take from an University”.

And if you are about to make a choice between University and Apprenticeship, then take a good week to think about it as either way can change your career-life drastically so do your research properly on this matter.
