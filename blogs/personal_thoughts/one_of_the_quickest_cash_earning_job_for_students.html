One of the Quickest Cash-Earning Job for Students

Posted on 18/02/2019

As a University student, it can be difficult to keep hold of your money in your pocket. I understand the needs and urges as it comes costly and this makes it harder for a young person to manage their spending even if you’re receiving student finance. Consequently, the more money you have the more you spend hence you become broke within 2 weeks. So, most likely, you will probably look for a job to earn extra cash so you survive for the month.

There are variety of jobs, mostly in retail however in my experience I would suggest the security industry. Whether as a steward or a door supervisor, you can benefit far from only earning cash. Stewarding doesn’t require any training but you will earn £1-2 less than a door supervisor and have less responsibility. On the other hand, in order to work as a door supervisor, you need to pass an exam and get your certificate which in total costs £220, but you can earn minimum £8 per hour as a start and get up to £10 in various places and companies.

With these positions, you can work as a part time with long hour shifts so you can earn more. If you don’t choose to work in a bar/pub/club and instead work in various events and/or festivals then you will comprehend as to why I’m writing this post. Because I don’t think you will want to deal with dumb people fighting every night. So, depending on the workplace and what sort of work you will be given, you will either search bags, check id’s, protect a door or wander about for any suspicious activities. Oh, and I mustn’t forget the response team, these guys as the name suggests respond to any fights occurring but most of their shift will be bossing around and act tough to feed their fat ass egos which you shouldn’t respect.

However, the most fun part begins if you find to work in events, concerts and festivals. Not only it’s free, you also earn good amount of cash whilst feeling the atmosphere and meeting new people. If you aren’t miserable, you will actually have a great time working because trust me, it’s fun to be around lively places and people. As you meet many people and colleagues from variety of places, background and culture, networking comes into play and opportunities open by. You will have a greater chance of finding help with your course and future than any other part-time job in my opinion.

Also, if you look at it in a positive way of working long hours standing, this will make you more durable over time and force you to stay active. Therefore, I reckon working in a security industry as a part-time job (not for career) can be beneficial in multiple ways for a student.
